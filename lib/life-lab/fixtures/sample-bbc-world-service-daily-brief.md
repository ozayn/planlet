## Vocabulary and phrasing

### gateway island

**Meaning:** a place that functions as an entry point into a larger political or geographic system.

**Why the wording matters:** it frames Lampedusa not just as a place, but as a symbol of Europe's border crisis.

**Example:** Officials described the gateway island as the first test of whether reception systems could absorb summer arrivals.

### final farewell

**Meaning:** language that presents an ending as solemn, complete, and publicly shared.

**Why the wording matters:** it turns a leader's death into a ritual obligation for the nation.

**Example:** State television called the ceremony a final farewell that would guide the country's next chapter.

### coalition arithmetic

**Meaning:** the practical count of seats and alliances needed to govern.

**Why the wording matters:** it shifts politics from ideology to institutional math.

**Example:** Coalition arithmetic now matters more than manifesto promises.

### heat exposure gap

**Meaning:** measurable difference in how heat risk affects different neighborhoods or groups.

**Why the wording matters:** it makes inequality visible in environmental terms.

**Example:** The report highlighted a heat exposure gap between coastal and inland districts.
